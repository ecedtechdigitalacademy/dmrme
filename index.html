SECTION A

(20 MARKS)

[Compulsory]

  1. (a) During Religious and Moral Education lessons, Sir Lalas quoted, "It is a biological process of maturation, a significant milestone in an individual's life. Across diverse cultures, societies have developed various rituals to commemorate this transition from childhood to adulthood. These rites often hold deep cultural and social significance, serving to prepare and welcome young individuals into their roles as adults within their communities."

    1. In your view, to which rite is Sir Lalas referring?

      Answer: Sir Lalas is referring to the puberty rite, which marks the transition from childhood to adulthood.

      [2 marks]

    2. In your opinion, state four emotional changes that occur during the rite mentioned in 1(a)(i).

      Answer:

      • Increased sense of responsibility
      • Feelings of independence
      • Anxiety or nervousness about new roles
      • Pride and accomplishment

      [8 marks]

    3. In what four ways is the rite significant to individuals?

      Answer:

      • Marks the individual's acceptance into adult society
      • Provides a sense of identity and belonging
      • Facilitates personal growth and self-awareness
      • Reinforces cultural values and traditions

      [8 marks]

    4. Mention two examples of the rite in Ghana.

      Answer:

      • Dipo ceremony among the Krobo people
      • Bragoro puberty rites among the Ashanti people

      [2 marks]

SECTION B

[40 MARKS]

Answer two questions only from this section.

  1. (a) Akordia defined moral attributes of God as attributes that God shares with humans, which society expects from us. Identify the kind of moral attribute demonstrated in the statement below.

    1. Fiifi beat Amina. But when the case was sent to the Boss, he settled it amicably and with fairness.

      Answer: Justice

      [2 marks]

    2. Otu is good to me. He helps me with his unused clothing and sometimes dashes me some money.

      Answer: Generosity

      [2 marks]

    3. Pinto had to wait for hours before it reached his turn. He never got annoyed.

      Answer: Patience

      [2 marks]

    (b) Mumuni and Francis belong to different religions, in what four ways do they share similar attributes of God?

    Answer:

    • Both believe in a compassionate God
    • Both believe in a just God
    • Both believe in a merciful God
    • Both believe in a God who rewards good deeds

    [8 marks]

    (c) With two examples, explain why God is given the following attributes:

    1. Sustainer:

      Answer: God provides for all the needs of His creations, ensuring their survival and well-being. For instance, He provides food and shelter.

    2. Merciful:

      Answer: God forgives sins and shows compassion towards His creations. An example is the story of the prodigal son.

    3. Dependable:

      Answer: God is always reliable and faithful, keeping His promises. An example is the fulfillment of the covenant with Abraham.

    [6 marks]

  2. (a) How do you understand the concepts of 'good deeds' and 'rewards'?

    Answer: Good deeds are positive actions taken to help others, show kindness, or follow moral principles. Rewards are benefits or recognition received in return for performing good deeds.

    [4 marks]

    (b) In your view, outline four important reasons for rewarding people.

    Answer:

    • Encourages positive behavior
    • Motivates continued good actions
    • Recognizes and values contributions
    • Promotes a culture of appreciation

    [8 marks]

    (c) Addo feels remorseful for not studying hard and failing to pass his Basic Education Certificate Examination.

    1. What steps did he go through to reach this state of remorse?

      Answer:

      • Realization of failure
      • Reflection on his lack of effort
      • Acknowledgment of the consequences
      • Feeling regret and guilt

      [8 marks]
